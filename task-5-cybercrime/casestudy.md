# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, where attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details. With the rapid growth of digital payments, such frauds have become increasingly frequent, especially among young users.

A typical UPI fraud begins when the victim receives a call, SMS, or WhatsApp message from someone pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer or seller from an online marketplace. The attacker builds trust by sounding professional and convincing. They then send a “collect request” or ask the victim to scan a QR code. They often claim that scanning the code will help receive money, but in reality, it authorizes a payment from the victim’s account.

In some cases, the victim is asked to share an OTP or UPI PIN under the pretext of verification. Once the victim approves the request or shares these sensitive details, money is instantly transferred to the attacker’s account without their full understanding.

College students and young digital users are frequent targets due to their high usage of online payment apps and limited awareness of such scams.

The consequences include immediate financial loss, difficulty in recovering funds, and emotional stress. Since UPI transactions are instant, reversing them is often very challenging.

This case highlights the importance of awareness, verifying requests, and never sharing confidential financial information online.
